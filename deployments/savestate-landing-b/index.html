Yes! One of SaveState's most powerful features is cross-snapshot search. SAF stands for SaveState Archive Format ‚Äî an open specification for packaging AI state. It organizes your data into structured directories: identity (personality, config, tools), memory (core memories, knowledge), conversations (threads, metadata), and meta (platform info, snapshot chain). The format is versioned, documented, and open source. No vendor lock-in ‚Äî even for your backup tool.
That's one of SaveState's core features. Take a snapshot from one platform and restore it to another: savestate restore v12 --to claude. SaveState maps data between platform formats ‚Äî memories, instructions, and configurations get translated intelligently. The open SAF format acts as the universal intermediary between platforms.
